I am currently working with Dr. Huanbo Sun at Peking University on robot learning. I received my M.S. degree from the University of Southern California, where I was fortunate to be advised by Dr. Heather Culbertson and work closely with Dr. Erdem Biyik. I earned my B.Eng. from the School of Computer and Information Technology at Beijing Jiaotong University, where I was advised by Dr. Youfang Lin and Sheng Han.

I have previously spent wonderful summers at BJTU INSIS and MIT CSGF, and was fortunate to intern at CASIA and Lenovo. These experiences shaped my interests in reinforcement learning and robotics. My long-term research goal is to enable robots to better understand and interact with the physical world. Specifically, I aim to develop more robust robot learning systems using multimodal inputs, thereby enhancing robotic capabilities and ultimately improving people’s lives.

Beyond research, I have volunteered as a teacher in both China and the United States, and also as a volunteer at the Beijing Zoo—experiences I truly cherish. Outside the lab, I enjoy piano, badminton, and Chinese classical poetry.

USC HaRVI Lab (Haptics Robotics and Virtual Interaction)
January 2024 – December 2024 Los Angeles, California

Advised by Dr. Heather Culbertson, collaborated with Dr. Erdem Bıyık.

  • Developed a preference-learning–based haptic recommendation system that performs interactive search, leveraging human preference feedback and model uncertainty to learn the latent distribution of users’ haptic preferences.
  • Developed a hand-tracking and control module for a 6-DoF motion-platform VR surfing system, fusing hand kinematics with surfboard motion control to enhance interaction naturalness and VR presence.
