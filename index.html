Rongtao Zhang

张镕韬 | Master Student

University of Southern California

I am a second-year M.S. student at the University of Southern California, conducting research in the HaRVI Lab. I received my B.Eng. from the School of Computer and Information Technology at Beijing Jiaotong University.

I’ve previously spent wonderful summers at BJTU INSIS and MIT CSGF, and was fortunate to intern at CASIA and Lenovo. These experiences shaped my interests in reinforcement learning and robotics.

My current research focuses on leveraging multimodal inputs to build more capable robot learning systems that can meaningfully improve people’s lives. I am also interested in human–robot interaction and extended reality (XR) systems.

Beyond research, I have volunteered as a teacher in both China and the United States, and as a volunteer at the Beijing Zoo—experiences I deeply cherish.

Outside the lab, I enjoy piano, writing, and Chinese classical poetry.

Research Intern
USC HaRVI Lab (Haptics Robotics and Virtual Interaction)
January 2024 – December 2024 Los Angeles, California

Advised by Dr. Heather Culbertson, collaborated with Dr. Erdem Bıyık.

  • Developed a preference-learning–based haptic recommendation system that performs interactive search, leveraging human preference feedback and model uncertainty to learn the latent distribution of users’ haptic preferences.
  • Developed a hand-tracking and control module for a 6-DoF motion-platform VR surfing system, fusing hand kinematics with surfboard motion control to enhance interaction naturalness and VR presence.
  • Implemented a vision-tracked haptic feedback module for a telepresence social interaction system, enabling remote interaction via a wearable haptic glove with haptic cues synchronized to visual tracking.
